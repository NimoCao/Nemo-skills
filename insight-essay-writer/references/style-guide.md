# Nemo Insight Essay Style Guide

Use this reference when writing or polishing a Chinese judgment-driven essay.

## What This Style Is

The essay should feel like a thoughtful builder/creator talking through a judgment they have earned from practice.

It is not hot-take writing. It does not win by being loud. It wins by making a distinction the reader did not have before.

Good default tone:

- clear but not smug
- critical but not dismissive
- reflective but not vague
- concrete but not simplistic
- opinionated but fair
- lightly literary only at key moments

## Literary Texture

This style can have a little literary feeling, but it should never become lyrical for its own sake.

Use literary texture only when it does real work:

- to make a key judgment more memorable
- to soften a landing after a sharp critique
- to give the ending a small aftertaste
- to make an abstract shift feel concrete

Good:

```text
它不是一直站在舞台中央，更像一层环境里的智能。平时很安静，需要时很近。
```

This works because the image clarifies the argument.

Bad:

```text
在时代的潮水里，Agent 像一束穿过迷雾的光，照亮人类生产力的远方。
```

This is too grand, too decorative, and not anchored in the user's actual judgment.

Rule of thumb: 90% of the essay should be clear reasoning and concrete scenes. The remaining 10% can carry a little aesthetic pressure, especially near pivots and endings.

## Argument Pattern

Strong essays often follow this progression:

1. **Phenomenon**: “最近我看到...” / “很多人现在...” / “我越来越觉得...”
2. **Surface description**: Describe what people are doing in plain terms.
3. **Acknowledgment**: Say why this behavior makes sense or why it has value.
4. **Turn**: “但我还是会有一个感觉...” / “问题在于...” / “真正难的不是...”
5. **Core distinction**: Define the difference that matters.
6. **Deeper reason**: Explain the underlying production mode, workflow, incentive, user behavior, or attention cost.
7. **Concrete examples**: Use work scenes, product use, writing workflows, files, screens, meetings, mobile moments, feedback, drafts.
8. **Standard**: Offer 2-3 questions or criteria to judge the thing.
9. **Closing**: Return to the central sentence with restraint.

## Sentence Granularity

Avoid writing every sentence as its own paragraph.

Use this rhythm:

- 2-5 sentence paragraphs for explanation.
- One-line paragraphs for pivots, emotional weight, or thesis statements.
- Semicolons or short parallel clauses for compact comparisons.
- Lists only when they genuinely help scan a framework.

Bad rhythm:

```text
OS 代表入口。
代表上下文。
代表权限。
代表记忆。
```

Better rhythm:

```text
OS 代表入口、上下文、权限、记忆，以及用户每天都会经过的操作环境。
```

Keep some short lines for emphasis:

```text
先别急着叫 Agent OS。
```

## Useful Moves

### The Fair Critique

First acknowledge the ambition, then raise the standard.

```text
这个野心本身没有问题。甚至我觉得，这是正确的方向。问题在于，越是想叫 OS，越不能只停留在 OS 的叙事上。
```

### The Deeper Reframe

Move from feature-level critique to production-mode critique.

```text
如果 Agent 真的成立，它改变的不是某个按钮，而是人和软件之间的生产关系。
```

### The Cost Lens

Analyze products through cost allocation:

- attention cost
- context organization cost
- switching cost
- judgment cost
- feedback cost
- coordination cost

### The Better Standard

Turn criticism into criteria:

```text
判断一个 Agent OS，不看它有多少 Agent，而看它有没有降低沟通成本，以及有没有形成持续理解。
```

## Common Structures

### Concept Critique Essay

Use when the user wants to critique a product/category/industry term.

```text
Title

Observed phenomenon.
Why it is understandable.
Why the current framing is not enough.

Section: why everyone wants this framing
Section: what the real standard should be
Section: where current products fall short
Section: better direction
Closing judgment
```

### Personal Method Essay

Use when the user wants to explain a personal workflow or belief.

```text
Title

Personal observation or recent trigger.
Old assumption.
New realization.
Concrete workflow or example.
Principle behind it.
What readers can take away.
```

## Avoid

- Do not overuse rhetorical questions.
- Do not make the piece too literary; excessive metaphor, atmosphere, or “beautiful sentences” will feel sentimental and fake.
- Do not use “首先/其次/最后” unless the piece is explicitly instructional.
- Do not turn every insight into a numbered framework.
- Do not make the conclusion grander than the argument supports.
- Do not flatten the user's judgment into generic advice.
- Do not write like a VC memo unless the user asks for that tone.
- Do not over-polish into corporate language.

## Final Pass Checklist

Before delivering:

- The first 3-5 paragraphs clearly show what triggered the piece.
- The article explains why the criticized behavior is tempting.
- The core judgment can be summarized in one sentence.
- Explanations are not broken into too many one-line paragraphs.
- The piece has at least two concrete scenes or examples.
- The ending lands softly but clearly.
